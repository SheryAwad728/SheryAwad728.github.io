Research

Border Effects in Social Connectedness

SSRN · May 2026

Shery Awad  ·  May 2026  ·  California State University, Long Beach

I examine the effect of state borders on social connectedness using a gravity framework applied to U.S. county-to-county Facebook friendship data. Crossing a state border reduces social connectedness by approximately 62%, even after controlling for geographic distance and contiguity. I test four mechanisms: average undergraduate enrollment, flagship university presence and catchment areas, shared commuting zones, and political differences. Counties hosting a flagship and those with higher enrollment exhibit weaker within-state ties, consistent with a national orientation effect. Shared commuting zones emerge as the strongest mechanism — within-state pairs sharing a local economic community show significantly stronger social ties. Political dissimilarity reduces social connectedness and amplifies the cross-border penalty. The border effect remains large and significant across all specifications, suggesting additional sources of friction remain unidentified.
